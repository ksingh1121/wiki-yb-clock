# Takamoto et al. Nature 2005

**Summary**: The first demonstration of a magic-wavelength optical lattice clock, using ⁸⁷Sr atoms trapped at 813.4 nm with a measured clock frequency of 429,228,004,229,952(15) Hz — establishing the optical lattice clock concept.

**Sources**: Takamoto et al. nature 2005.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-04-26

---

## Reference

M. Takamoto, F.-L. Hong, R. Higashi, and H. Katori, "An optical lattice clock," *Nature* **435**, 321–324 (2005).

## Context and Significance

This paper represents the first experimental realization of the optical lattice clock concept originally proposed by Katori (2002). The key idea — that a "magic wavelength" exists at which both clock states experience identical AC Stark shifts, making the clock transition immune to trap-depth fluctuations — was here proven by experiment. Prior to this work, optical clocks used either free atoms (first-order Doppler limited) or single trapped ions (QPN limited by single-atom signal). The lattice clock offers N-atom averaging while maintaining Lamb-Dicke confinement.

## Key Results

- **Species**: ⁸⁷Sr (I=9/2 fermion)
- **Clock transition**: ¹S₀(F=9/2) → ³P₀(F=9/2) at ~698 nm (clock frequency ≈429 THz)
- **Magic wavelength**: 813.420(7) nm — first experimental determination
- **Measured clock frequency**: 429,228,004,229,952(15) Hz (absolute, via optical frequency comb against H-maser + GPS)
- **Frequency uncertainty**: 15 Hz (~3.5×10⁻¹⁴), dominated by systematic shifts
- **Trap depth**: ~800 $E_r$, demonstrating magic-wavelength compensation of light shift at that level

## Experimental Method

1. ⁸⁷Sr atoms cooled in a two-stage MOT (461 nm + 689 nm) to ~2 µK
2. Loaded into a 1D optical lattice at the candidate magic wavelength (813 nm)
3. Clock transition driven by a 698 nm laser (narrow linewidth, locked to ULE cavity)
4. Excitation fraction measured vs. lattice depth at two lattice wavelengths to locate $\Delta\alpha(\nu_L)=0$
5. Absolute frequency measured by optical frequency comb referenced to a H-maser + GPS receiver

## Magic Wavelength Determination

The slope $d(\delta\nu)/dU$ was measured at two lattice wavelengths and compared. The magic wavelength was located where this slope changes sign. The measurement accuracy was limited by the wavelength measurement of the 813 nm lattice laser (wavemeter calibration). The value 813.420(7) nm agreed with theoretical predictions and established the proof of principle.

## Stability

Measured short-term instability: $\sigma_y(\tau) \approx 1.5\times10^{-15}/\sqrt{\tau}$, limited by Dick effect and modest atom numbers. The fractional frequency was determined at $\pm 15$ Hz over the measurement, with subsequent evaluation expected to reach $10^{-17}$.

## Broader Impact

This paper established the optical lattice clock architecture that all subsequent lattice clock groups (NIST Yb, PTB Sr, SYRTE Sr, NMIJ Yb, ECNU Yb, etc.) built upon. The Yb magic wavelength work at NIST followed directly.
