## Paper Summaries

### Foundational Papers
- [[takamoto-nature-2005]] — First magic-wavelength OLC using ⁸⁷Sr; magic λ = 813.420(7) nm (Katori group)
- [[hoyt-prl-2005]] — First Yb 1S₀→3P₀ spectroscopy in MOT; ¹⁷¹Yb at 518,295,836,591.6(4.4) kHz (NIST)

### Early Yb Lattice Clocks
- [[barber-prl-2006]] — First ¹⁷⁴Yb lattice clock with MIS; magic λ = 759.35(0.02) nm (NIST)
- [[barber-prl-2008]] — Lattice light shifts in Yb; hyperpolarizability 170(33) mHz at 500 E_r (NIST)
- [[lemke-prl-2009]] — First ¹⁷¹Yb lattice clock; freq 518,295,836,590,865.2(0.7) Hz; 3.4×10⁻¹⁶ uncertainty (NIST)
- [[kohno-ape-2009]] — Independent ¹⁷¹Yb lattice clock at NMIJ; 28 Hz uncertainty; SFG clock laser

### Systematic Reduction Milestones
- [[sherman-prl-2012]] — DC Stark measurement of Δα(0)=36.2612(7) kHz(kV/cm)⁻²; BBR to 3×10⁻¹⁷ (NIST)
- [[beloy-prl-2014]] — In-vacuum copper BBR shield; dynamic correction η₁, η₂; BBR to 10⁻¹⁸ (NIST)
- [[brown-prl-2017]] — Operational magic wavelength; β*=−5.5(2)×10⁻²²; lattice shift to 1.2×10⁻¹⁸ (NIST)
- [[gao-sr-2018]] — Synchronous comparison at ECNU; 1.7×10⁻¹⁶ total; density shift −0.570(25) Hz

### Theory and Proposals
- [[safronova-prl-2018]] — Dual clock: 3P₀→J=2 at 1695 nm with K_α=−15 for α-variation search
- [[dzuba-pra-2018]] — CIPT calculations for multiple Yb clock transition candidates, 4f-hole state properties
- [[ludlow-rmp-2015]] — Comprehensive review of optical atomic clocks as of 2015

### Latest Evaluation
- [[bothwell-prl-2025]] — Dual-ensemble + running wave; ν_E1=394,798,266.9(2.6) MHz; M1E2 discrepancy resolved; all lattice shifts <10⁻¹⁸
